Growth Strategy

Four Vectors to Behavioral Health Market Leadership

NFS's growth engine compounds across four coordinated vectors — geographic expansion, service line depth, payer mix optimization, and telehealth scaling — each building on the organization's 20+ year clinical foundation.

◆
Geographic Expansion
  • ›Expand from 4 to 10+ states by 2029 — CT, NJ, PA, VT, ME as priority markets
  • ›Leverage telehealth to establish presence before physical office opens
  • ›Target Medicaid-heavy markets with unmet behavioral health demand
  • ›Evaluate state licensure timelines and regulatory pathways for each market
  • ›Partner with community organizations and schools for rapid community trust
◈
Payer & Referral Relationships
  • ›Expand and strengthen MCO (Managed Care Organization) contracts in all states
  • ›Build school district, pediatrician, and primary care referral networks
  • ›Develop hospital and ED diversion partnerships for crisis referrals
  • ›Pursue value-based care contracts that reward outcomes over volume
  • ›Pursue state agency contracts (DCF, DDS, school systems) in each new market
◉
Service Line Innovation
  • ›Scale ABA nationally — highest growth behavioral health segment
  • ›Launch school-based behavioral health program across all active states
  • ›Develop integrated care model: therapy + medication management + care coordination
  • ›Add crisis stabilization / intensive outpatient (IOP) program
  • ›Explore substance use disorder (SUD) services — highest state funding priority
◐
Telehealth Platform Scaling
  • ›Grow telehealth from complement to primary growth driver — unlimited geographic reach
  • ›Invest in telehealth UX, intake automation, and clinician matching technology
  • ›Expand telehealth into school-based and employer assistance program (EAP) markets
  • ›Build telehealth-first model for new state entries before physical offices open
  • ›Develop proprietary outcomes tracking to differentiate from digital-only competitors

Partnership Development Strategy
Primary Referral
School Districts

Multi-year contracts for school-based mental health services. Each district = hundreds of client referrals and a stable Medicaid billing stream.

Payer Contract
Managed Care Orgs

Medicaid MCO preferred provider agreements in each state. Network inclusion drives volume — every MCO contract unlocks thousands of eligible members.

Clinical Referral
Pediatricians & PCPs

Warm referral relationships with pediatric and primary care practices. Co-location and integrated care agreements create a steady referral pipeline.

System Partner

ED diversion agreements and discharge planning partnerships. Hospitals are overwhelmed with behavioral health — NFS is the community-based solution they need.

Grant Funding
State Agencies (DCF/DDS)

State child welfare, developmental disability, and substance use agencies. Contracts with DCF/DDS provide both volume and mission-aligned funding.

Employer Channel
EAP Programs

Employee Assistance Program partnerships with regional employers. Commercial payer revenue diversification and higher reimbursement rates than Medicaid.
